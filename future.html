Module I // Informational Influx

Radio Astronomy & Signal Geometry

A radio telescope operates by collecting extremely faint, low-frequency electromagnetic radiation originating from deep space sources. Information transverses the cosmos as planar wavefronts.

Antenna Theorem (Effective Area)

$$ A_e = \frac{\lambda^2}{4\pi} G $$

The massive parabolic dish acts as a primary reflector, rigorously reflecting the incoming parallel planar waves vectors down to a single focal point—a sub-reflector housing the feed horn.

Module II // Extreme Stellar Remnants

Pulsar Rotational Kinetics

Following supernova core collapse, conservation of angular momentum forces the resulting neutron star to spin at extreme velocities. This forms a Pulsar—a highly magnetized, rigid body rotating in a vacuum.

Rotational Kinetic Energy

$$ K_{rot} = \frac{1}{2} I \omega^2 $$

Magnetic Dipole Radiation Power

$$ P = \frac{\mu_0 m^2 \omega^4}{6\pi c^3} \sin^2\alpha $$

The misalignment angle $\alpha$ between the rotation axis and the magnetic dipole axis emits beams of electromagnetic radiation.

Module III // Theoretical Symmetries

Noether's Theorem & Conservation Laws

Emmy Noether's profound theorem states that every differentiable symmetry of the action of a physical system generates a corresponding conservation law.

Angular Momentum Conservation

$$ \text{If } \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \theta} = 0, \text{ then } \frac{d}{dt}\left(\frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \dot{\theta}}\right) = 0 $$ $$ \implies \mathbf{L} = \mathbf{r} \times \mathbf{p} = \text{constant} $$

A continuous spatial rotational symmetry analytically proves that a system's angular momentum $\mathbf{L}$ remains strictly conserved over time independently of specific internal forces.
